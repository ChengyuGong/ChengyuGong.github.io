Vector Databases

High-Performance and Data-Intensive Computing (HPDIC) research lab

Jan 2024 - Present · 9 mos

In my recent research project, I tackled a critical challenge in scientific data management by developing the Order-Preserving Dimension Reduction (OPDR) method to efficiently process k-nearest neighbors queries in multimodal datasets. This method significantly reduces the dimensionality of embedding vectors while maintaining accurate relational data in a reduced-dimensional space. I successfully formulated a quantitative function that elucidates the correlation between reduced dimensions and other key variables, culminating in a preprint. Our experiments verified that OPDR effectively manages high-dimensional data, enhancing performance in time-sensitive scientific applications.

Our forthcoming research will concentrate on investigating the applications of Entropic Multimodality in Vector Databases. This innovative research initiative is designed to leverage the principles of Entropic Multimodality to optimize the processing efficiency and precision of vector databases during complex query execution and data interactions.

Advanced Decision-Making Systems Lab

Nov 2023 - Present · 11 mos

In my current research, I explore how large language models (LLMs) like GPT-3.5 evaluate and generate ideas, focusing on their application in venture capital decision-making. This involves training models on extensive datasets to capture the subtle patterns of successful investment strategies.
